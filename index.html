About Andres

A book born from
real leadership.

"I did not set out to write a leadership book. I set out to be a better leader. And somewhere along that journey, I realized that the thing I was learning was the thing most people in leadership never get to learn at all."

— Andres Vera, Introduction

It started years ago with his first leadership role — a coordinator position back in his home country. No playbook, no mentor, no one pulling him aside to say — here is what you need to know. Just a title, a team, and the full weight of figuring it out completely alone.

Over the years, Andres moved through role after role, each one adding a new layer — new pressures, new team dynamics, new lessons about what actually moves people. Not the big gestures. The small ones. The consistency. The follow-through. The moment you choose to recognize someone when it would have been easier not to.

Today he holds a leadership position and is currently pursuing his MBA in Management and Leadership. He is also fortunate to be surrounded by people who are actively making him better — mentors, colleagues, and leaders who are still shaping who he is becoming. That ongoing growth is part of what makes this book honest.

This book is for the younger version of himself — the one who stepped into that coordinator role years ago with no support, no guidance, and no one saying the things that could have made those early years so much less difficult. It is the book Andres wishes had existed when he needed it most. And it is the support he hopes every new leader today gets before they ever have to figure it out alone.

What's Inside

The principles that
actually change teams.

Each chapter is built around a core dimension of leadership — from leading yourself before leading anyone else, to connecting the daily work to a bigger sense of purpose. Every insight comes from real experience, not a textbook.

Chapter 1

Leading Yourself First

"The most important person you will ever manage is you."

Before you can lead anyone else, you have to be able to lead yourself. Self-leadership is not a soft skill — it is the foundation everything else is built on.

Chapter 2

Trust Is Built in Small Moments

"Authority is given. Trust is earned. Never confuse the two."

Trust lives in whether you remember what someone told you in passing, whether you follow through on small commitments, and whether you give credit generously.

Chapter 3

Communication That Connects

"What doesn't get communicated doesn't exist."

Most leaders are waiting to talk, not listening to understand. Real listening — full presence, follow-up questions, no interruptions — is what builds the real version of your team.

Chapter 4

Motivation Through Recognition

"The ones who work hardest are often the least recognized."

Recognition is not a reward program. It is the daily practice of actually seeing your people — specifically, personally, and in the right moment — before they quietly decide to stop giving everything they have.

Chapter 5

Handling Challenges with Grace

"Calm is a leadership decision."

Your emotional register sets the ceiling for your team's response. Grace under pressure is not a personality trait — it is a set of choices, built through practice, one difficult moment at a time.

Chapters 6 & 7

Growing People & Holding Vision

"The best thing you will ever build is not a result. It is a person."

Developing others and holding a clear sense of purpose are what turn a group of people doing work into a team that believes in something — and a leader people actually remember.
